Research Vision

I am an observational astrophysicist exploring the cosmic history of star formation[cite: 516, 517]. My research integrates multi-wavelength data to trace the evolution of the star-formation rate density ($\rho_{SFR}$) from the present day back to the reionization era ($z \sim 11$)[cite: 517, 527].

I am particularly focused on the dust-obscured side of the early Universe, utilizing high-resolution observations from JWST, ALMA, and SCUBA-2[cite: 521, 528, 685].

Leading Programs

NCN SONATA BIS

Evolution of Star Formation with JWST

Establishing a research group to analyze the first large-area JWST PRIMER survey, characterizing star-forming properties of ~120,000 early galaxies[cite: 527, 528, 529].

NCN SONATA

Time Evolution of the SFRD

Determining the direct and indirect contribution of the brightest and faintest far-IR sources to the cosmic star formation history[cite: 91, 95, 97].
