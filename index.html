Exploring Galaxy Evolution

From Cosmic Dawn to the Present Day

Research Objectives

My work focuses on completing our knowledge of the cosmic history of star formation[cite: 13, 516]. I utilize deep multi-wavelength imaging to investigate the star formation rate density ($\rho_{SFR}$) and the luminosity function (LF) of galaxies[cite: 15, 517, 519].

Key research areas include[cite: 532, 533, 534]:

  • Evolution of the star-forming main sequence up to $z \sim 11$[cite: 532, 752].
  • Far-IR properties, gas depletion timescales, and gas mass fractions of quiescent galaxies[cite: 532, 880, 882].
  • Calibration of IR luminosity relationships ($IRX-\beta$ and $IRX-M_*$)[cite: 533, 943, 947].
  • Dust properties, including temperature, emissivity, and attenuation curves[cite: 534, 535, 992].

Leading Research Programs

NCN SONATA BIS

Evolution of Star Formation in the Early Universe with JWST

This project utilizes unique JWST NIRCam+MIRI imaging from the PRIMER survey to revolutionize our knowledge of star formation out to $z \sim 12$[cite: 528, 529]. We focus on identifying ~120,000 galaxies to trace the dust-enshrouded star formation in the reionization era[cite: 529, 695, 701].

NCN SONATA

Time Evolution of the SFRD in the Early Universe

A detailed analysis of the star formation history using state-of-the-art data sets from ALMA (AS2UDS) and SCUBA-2[cite: 91, 92, 254]. We apply novel stacking techniques to determine the IR luminosity for even the faintest galaxies in the field[cite: 93, 260, 274].
