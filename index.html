Exploring the Early Universe

Unveiling the dust-enshrouded history of cosmic star formation.

Research Vision

My research addresses one of the most fundamental challenges in modern astrophysical cosmology: reconstructing the complete cosmic history of star formation[cite: 13, 516]. While UV observations have reached the highest redshifts, half of the Universe's stellar emission remains hidden by cosmic dust[cite: 17, 521].

By leveraging the transformational power of JWST, ALMA, and SCUBA-2, my group investigates the evolution of the star formation rate density (ρSFR) and the physical properties of galaxies from the present day back to the reionization era ($z \sim 11$)[cite: 527, 695].

Leading Research Programs

NCN SONATA BIS

Evolution of Star Formation in the Early Universe with JWST

Establishing a research team to analyze the first blank-field, large-area survey from the James Webb Space Telescope (PRIMER)[cite: 527, 528]. We focus on the star-forming main sequence, gas mass fractions, and dust properties of the first galaxies[cite: 531, 532, 534].

NCN SONATA

Cosmic History of Star Formation Rate Density

Direct determination of the SFRD out to $z > 5$ using state-of-the-art sub-mm imaging from ALMA (AS2UDS) and JCMT/SCUBA-2[cite: 91, 95, 254]. This project utilizes novel stacking techniques to account for even the faintest galaxies in the early Universe[cite: 93, 260].
